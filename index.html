Now, I am going to describe my home country Nepal. Nepal is a landlocked country located in South Asia, between India and China. It is known for its natural beauty, mountains, cultural diversity, and rich traditions. Nepal is home to Mount Everest, the highest mountain in the world. The capital city is Kathmandu, and the country has many ethnic groups, languages, and religions living together peacefully.

Flag of Nepal: Nepal’s flag is unique because it is the only non-rectangular national flag in the world. It has two triangular shapes stacked one above the other. The flag is crimson red with a blue border. The upper triangle contains a moon, and the lower triangle contains a sun, symbolizing peace, hope, and the long-lasting existence of Nepal.

Prithvi Narayan Shah was the founder of modern Nepal. He was the King of Gorkha who unified many small kingdoms into one nation in the 18th century. Through his leadership, bravery, and strategic planning, he successfully conquered the Kathmandu Valley and other regions, laying the foundation of present-day Nepal. He is remembered as a visionary ruler who promoted national unity and independence.

There are three regions of Nepal:

1. Mountain Region (Himalayan Region): Located in the north, this region includes the Himalayas and Mount Everest. It has cold weather, high altitude, and sparse population.

2. Hill Region: Situated between the mountains and plains, this region has valleys, hills, and moderate climate. Major cities like Kathmandu and Pokhara lie here.

❮ ❯

3. Terai Region: The southern lowland plains with fertile soil and warm climate. It is the main agricultural and densely populated area of Nepal.
